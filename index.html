Option Pricing with PINN (Physics-Informed Neural Network)

In this project, I developed a physics-informed neural network to replicate the Black-Scholes option pricing model. PINNs are a novel approach that combines the power of neural networks with the constraints of physical laws or mathematical equations.

What are PINNs?

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) are a class of deep learning models that incorporate physical laws into the learning process. They are designed to solve supervised learning tasks while respecting any given law of physics described by general nonlinear partial differential equations.

How PINNs Work in Option Pricing

In the context of option pricing, PINNs can be used to solve the Black-Scholes partial differential equation (PDE). The neural network is trained to satisfy both the PDE and the initial/boundary conditions of the option pricing problem. This approach allows for:

  • Accurate pricing of options across a wide range of market conditions
  • Faster computation compared to traditional numerical methods
  • The ability to handle complex, multi-dimensional problems

Advantages of Using PINNs for Option Pricing

  • Flexibility: Can handle various types of options and underlying assets
  • Efficiency: Once trained, can provide real-time pricing
  • Accuracy: Can achieve high accuracy, especially in regions where data is sparse
